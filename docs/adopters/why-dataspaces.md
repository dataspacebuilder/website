# Why Dataspaces?

> The business case for trusted data sharing

---

## The Data Sharing Dilemma

Every organization today faces a fundamental tension:

**You need to share data** to collaborate with partners, serve customers, and create value.

**But sharing data is risky** — once data leaves your control, you lose visibility into how it's used, copied, or shared further.

Traditional approaches force you to choose:
- **Share freely** → Risk loss of control, compliance issues, competitive exposure
- **Share restrictively** → Miss opportunities, slow down collaboration, frustrate partners

**Dataspaces resolve this tension** by enabling controlled sharing with technical guarantees.

---

## What Makes Dataspaces Different

### 1. Data Sovereignty by Design

| Old Way | Dataspace Way |
|---------|---------------|
| Send data, hope for the best | Access is controlled and auditable |
| Contracts are legal documents only | Policies are technically enforced |
| Revocation means asking nicely | Access can be revoked instantly |

**Your data remains under your control, even when shared.**

### 2. Standardized Trust

Instead of establishing trust manually with each partner, dataspaces use **Verifiable Credentials**:

```
Traditional:
  "Can we trust Acme Corp?"
  → Check references
  → Review certifications (PDF)
  → Legal review
  → Sign NDA
  → 3-6 months later: start sharing

Dataspace:
  "Can we trust Acme Corp?"
  → System checks their credentials automatically
  → ISO certification: ✅ Verified
  → Industry membership: ✅ Verified  
  → Access granted in seconds
```

### 3. Network Effects

Each new participant makes the network more valuable:

- **More data** becomes available
- **More partners** to collaborate with
- **Lower friction** for everyone
- **Standard integration** works with all

## Real-World Examples

### Automotive Supply Chain (Catena-X)

**Challenge:** OEMs need visibility into multi-tier supply chains but suppliers are reluctant to share sensitive data.

**Solution:** Dataspaces enable suppliers to share exactly what's needed (e.g., carbon footprint data) without exposing confidential business information.

**Result:** Industry-wide traceability for sustainability compliance.

### Manufacturing Collaboration (Manufacturing-X)

**Challenge:** Machine builders and operators need to share performance data for predictive maintenance but trust is difficult to establish.

**Solution:** Verifiable credentials prove compliance certifications; usage policies ensure data is only used for agreed purposes.

**Result:** Predictive maintenance services without data exposure risks.

### Aerospace & Defence (DECADE-X)

**Challenge:** Complex supply chains with strict security requirements make data sharing nearly impossible.

**Solution:** Sovereign data sharing with cryptographic trust and fine-grained access control.

**Result:** Resilient, trusted supply chain collaboration.

## Addressing Common Concerns

### "We already have APIs"
APIs are point-to-point. For each partner, you build custom integration. Dataspaces provide a **standard protocol** — build once, connect with any participant.

### "Legal contracts already cover this"
Legal contracts are enforced after the fact, in court. Dataspace policies are enforced **technically, in real-time**. The data simply won't flow if policies aren't met.

### "Our data is too sensitive"
That's exactly why dataspaces exist. You define **exactly** who can access, for what purpose, for how long. Access is auditable and revocable.

### "This sounds complex"
For end users, it's not. You interact through a dashboard—publishing data, discovering offers, accepting contracts. The complexity is handled by the platform.

### "Who else is using this?"
Major industry initiatives: Catena-X (automotive), Manufacturing-X (industrial), DECADE-X (aerospace), Gaia-X (cross-industry). Backed by BMW, Airbus, Siemens, SAP, and dozens more.

## The Regulatory Push

### EU Data Act (2025)

The [EU Data Act](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/data-act) creates legal requirements for:
- B2B data sharing in certain contexts
- Data portability rights
- Fair data access conditions

Organizations need **technical infrastructure** to comply—dataspaces provide it.

### Industry Mandates

Many industries are creating data sharing requirements:
- **Automotive**: Battery passport, carbon footprint reporting
- **Manufacturing**: Digital product passport
- **Healthcare**: Research data sharing
- **Finance**: Open banking/finance regulations
