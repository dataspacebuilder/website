# Market Landscape

> Understanding the dataspace ecosystem

---

## The Dataspace Movement

Dataspaces represent a fundamental shift in how organizations share data. Unlike traditional approaches (APIs, EDI, file sharing), dataspaces provide:

- **Standardized protocols** — Interoperability by design
- **Decentralized trust** — No central authority required
- **Sovereign control** — Data owners maintain authority

## Protocol Landscape

### Dataspace Protocol (DSP)

The core protocol for dataspace interactions:

- **Catalog Protocol** — Discover data offers
- **Contract Negotiation** — Agree on terms
- **Transfer Process** — Coordinate data movement

**Status:** DSP 2025-1 is stable and production-ready.

### Decentralized Claims Protocol (DCP)

Identity and trust management:

- **DID** — Decentralized Identifiers
- **Verifiable Credentials** — Provable claims
- **Presentation Exchange** — Sharing proofs

### Data Plane Signaling (DPS)

Decouples control and data planes for:

- Specialized performance
- Protocol flexibility
- Independent scaling

## Competitive Analysis

### Proprietary Solutions

| Approach | vs. Dataspaces |
|----------|----------------|
| **Traditional EDI** | Rigid, expensive, limited partners |
| **API platforms** | Point-to-point, no sovereignty |
| **Cloud marketplaces** | Platform lock-in, limited control |

### Open Standards Advantage

- No vendor lock-in
- Ecosystem interoperability
- Long-term sustainability
- Regulatory alignment
