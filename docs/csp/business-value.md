# Business Value for Cloud Service Providers

> Transform data sharing into a strategic revenue driver

---

## Executive Summary

Dataspace services represent a significant opportunity for Cloud Service Providers to:

1. **Capture new revenue** through managed services with recurring subscriptions
2. **Increase customer lifetime value** by becoming essential infrastructure for B2B data exchange
3. **Differentiate competitively** with European sovereignty and compliance capabilities

---

## Market Opportunity

### The Growing Demand

## Revenue Model Options

### Subscription Tiers

<!-- TODO: Develop detailed pricing examples -->

**Basic Tier**
- Single connector instance
- Standard data planes (HTTP, S3)
- Basic support
- *Target: SMEs with simple data sharing needs*

**Professional Tier**
- Multiple connector instances
- Industrial data planes
- Advanced monitoring
- Priority support
- *Target: Mid-market companies in industry dataspaces*

**Enterprise Tier**
- Dedicated resources
- Custom data plane development
- SLA guarantees
- Dedicated account management
- *Target: Large enterprises with complex requirements*

### Value-Based Pricing Elements

- Per-tenant monthly fee
- Data transfer volume pricing
- Premium features (advanced analytics, compliance reporting)
- Professional services (integration, customization)

## Competitive Positioning

### Against Hyperscalers

- **European sovereignty** — Data stays in your infrastructure
- **Specialized focus** — Deep expertise in dataspace protocols
- **Customer proximity** — Personalized support and customization

### Against Self-Hosted Solutions

- **Operational efficiency** — Amortized costs across tenants
- **Faster time-to-value** — Pre-configured, ready to use
- **Continuous updates** — Always on latest stable versions

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Pilot (Months 1-3)
- Select 3-5 pilot customers
- Deploy basic multi-tenant environment
- Validate value proposition and pricing

### Phase 2: Limited Availability (Months 4-6)
- Onboard early adopter customers
- Build operational playbooks
- Develop sales materials and training

### Phase 3: General Availability (Months 7+)
- Launch marketing campaigns
- Scale sales team
- Expand feature set based on feedback
